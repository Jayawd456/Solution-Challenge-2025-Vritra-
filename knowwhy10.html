Know Why: Global Vision Impairment

Understanding the 2.2 Billion People Affected Worldwide

The Stark Reality

2.2 billion people globally suffer from vision impairment, a staggering number that represents nearly one-quarter of the world's population. This isn't just a statisticâ€”it's a global health challenge with profound implications for individual lives and societal development.

Why Does This Happen?

Major Causes

  • Uncorrected refractive errors (myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism)
  • Cataracts, especially in aging populations
  • Glaucoma and diabetic retinopathy
  • Lack of access to eye care in developing regions

Key Risk Factors

  • Aging populations
  • Chronic health conditions like diabetes
  • Genetic predispositions
  • Limited healthcare access

Global Impact

Economic

Estimated annual global productivity loss of $411 billion due to vision impairment

Social

Reduced quality of life, limited educational and employment opportunities

Health

Increased risk of accidents, depression, and reduced independence

What Can Be Done?

Prevention

  • Regular eye examinations
  • Early detection of eye conditions
  • Managing chronic health conditions

Solutions

  • Affordable corrective eyewear
  • Advanced medical treatments
  • Global health initiatives

Data sourced from World Health Organization (WHO) Global Report on Vision
