Know Why: 27 Million People are Affected by Diabetes

The Diabetes Epidemic

Every year, 27 million people are diagnosed with diabetes, a chronic condition that affects the body's ability to process glucose (sugar). This staggering number highlights the global health challenge posed by this disease.

Why So Many People?

Lifestyle Factors

Sedentary lifestyles, poor diet, and increasing obesity rates contribute significantly to diabetes risk.

Genetic Predisposition

Family history and genetic factors increase the likelihood of developing diabetes.

Aging Population

As populations age, the risk of type 2 diabetes increases, contributing to higher diagnosis rates.

Types of Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

An autoimmune condition where the body doesn't produce insulin, typically diagnosed in children and young adults.

Type 2 Diabetes

The most common form, where the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough, often related to lifestyle and age.

Prevention and Management

  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in nutrients
  • Exercise regularly
  • Monitor blood sugar levels
  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Manage stress and maintain a healthy weight
